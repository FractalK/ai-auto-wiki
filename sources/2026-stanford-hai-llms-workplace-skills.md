Stanford researchers argue that large language models function effectively as practice partners and mentors for training workplace social skills like conflict resolution and empathy. The study reveals that while practicing with an AI increases a user's confidence, AI-generated feedback is critical for developing actual social competence. This approach offers a scalable solution for improving soft skills in professional and counseling environments. A notable limitation is that these simulations currently depend on intensive expert design and are intended to supplement rather than replace human peer training.
