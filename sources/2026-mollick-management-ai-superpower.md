Ethan Mollick makes the case that management expertise, specifically the skill of effective delegation, becomes a primary superpower as AI transitions into autonomous agents. He posits that traditional management frameworks serve as highly effective prompts, allowing experts to lead complex projects by trading human labor for AI processing and review time. Key findings suggest that advanced models can now match human expert performance on diverse professional tasks, provided the manager can accurately scope deliverables and recognize high-quality results. While these tools dramatically lower the barrier to prototyping and pivoting, success remains contingent on the human manager’s deep domain knowledge. A notable caveat is the "jagged frontier" of AI ability, which makes it difficult to predict performance on specific, highly specialized tasks without expert oversight.
