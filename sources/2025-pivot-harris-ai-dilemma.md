Tristan Harris, former Google design ethicist and co-founder of the Center for Humane Technology, argues that AI presents a categorically deeper risk than social media because generative AI operates at the level of language — the operating system of human society — and AGI would constitute a general-purpose optimization force across all domains. Harris frames social media as "first contact" with narrow misaligned AI, generative AI as "second contact," and AGI as the "third contact" all major labs are racing to achieve. He documents the AI companion threat through the Character.AI case, citing session durations averaging 60–90 minutes versus approximately 12–15 minutes for ChatGPT, and argues that AI companion systems shift from attention-hacking to attachment-hacking — targeting the psychological mechanism through which humans form self-esteem and internalize social norms. Harris proposes a framework of four regulatory red lines and draws on nuclear arms control and the Montreal Protocol as models for international coordination, noting that the Biden-Xi 2023–2024 meetings included a China-initiated agreement to prevent AI use in nuclear command and control systems as a precedent for adversarial coordination on existential AI risks.
