Based on a survey of 100 senior government technology executives across six countries (US, UK, India, Singapore, Australia, Germany) conducted July–September 2025 with Oxford Economics, this IBM Institute for Business Value research brief argues that government AI adoption is accelerating from experimentation to enterprise-wide deployment, driven by a prevailing view that the risk of inaction exceeds the risk of transformation. The report identifies critical readiness gaps in data preparation, workforce skills, and governance frameworks that constrain actual value realization despite rising investment, and projects that governments will nearly double AI's share of IT budgets by 2030 while shifting spending toward generative and agentic applications.
