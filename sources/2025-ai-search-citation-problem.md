A systematic evaluation by the Tow Center for Digital Journalism at Columbia University tested eight generative AI search tools against 20 news publishers using 1,600 queries, finding that chatbots collectively produced incorrect answers to more than 60 percent of queries, with individual error rates ranging from 37 percent (Perplexity) to 94 percent (Grok 3). The study found that premium models paradoxically showed higher overall error rates than free counterparts because they more frequently gave definitive wrong answers rather than declining to respond, that multiple platforms retrieved content from publishers who had blocked their crawlers via robots.txt, and that content licensing deals did not improve citation accuracy. URL fabrication was severe for Gemini and Grok 3, with 154 of 200 Grok 3 citations leading to error pages even when the correct article was identified.
