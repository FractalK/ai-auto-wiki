AI Explained reviews Claude Opus 4.7 six days after its release, covering benchmarks from multiple external evaluators alongside Anthropic's own system card data. The video documents that despite advances on most metrics, Opus 4.7 underperforms Opus 4.6 on BrowseComp (agentic web search) and underperforms Gemini 3 Flash on external OCR testing, and reports that adaptive thinking is now mandatory — the model autonomously determines inference depth, cannot be forced into extended thinking, and defaults to medium effort. The video critiques the Mythos 4× engineer productivity survey as methodologically weak (opt-in sample, measuring output volume rather than quality or time savings), and documents external security research (Vidok) showing other flagship models with appropriate scaffolding can reproduce many of the specific vulnerabilities Anthropic highlighted as Mythos-exclusive discoveries. Broader context includes Anthropic's compute constraints relative to OpenAI, the Dario Amodei and Greg Brockman personal rivalry, and the scale of AI infrastructure investment relative to historical U.S. megaprojects.
