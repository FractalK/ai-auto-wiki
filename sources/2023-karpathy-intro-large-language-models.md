Karpathy's November 2023 lecture provides a comprehensive technical introduction to large language models for a general audience, covering both the pretraining and fine-tuning pipeline and the emerging agentic deployment paradigm. The talk explains scaling laws, the System 1 vs. System 2 framework as a roadmap for future AI capability development, and the LLM-as-operating-system analogy for understanding multi-tool integration. Karpathy dedicates substantial coverage to security vulnerabilities inherent to LLM deployment — jailbreaks, prompt injection, and data poisoning — framing these as structural properties of current architectures rather than implementation-level bugs. The talk reflects the state of the field as of late 2023 and does not present original research; its value is pedagogical synthesis rather than novel empirical findings.
