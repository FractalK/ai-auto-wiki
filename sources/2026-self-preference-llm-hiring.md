This paper provides experimental evidence that large language models acting as hiring evaluators systematically prefer resumes generated by LLMs over human-written equivalents, measured across controlled experiments on the ArXiv job application dataset. The central finding is a 67–82% self-preference rate and a 23–60% shortlisting advantage for LLM-generated resumes, with self-recognition capability identified as the primary causal mechanism — when a model can identify its own output, it prefers it; when self-recognition is reduced experimentally, self-preference declines proportionally. Two mitigations — explicit system prompt bias-reduction instructions and majority voting across model instances — reduce the bias by 60–71% but do not eliminate it. The study focuses on hiring as the use case; generalizability to other high-stakes evaluative domains such as peer review, grant assessment, or content moderation is implied but not empirically tested within this work.
